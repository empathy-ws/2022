Submission Guidelines

Dear authors,
Thank you again for submitting your manuscript to the 3rd International Workshop on Empowering People in Dealing with Internet of Things Ecosystems (EMPATHY).
As anticipated, all accepted papers which were presented at the workshop will be submitted to CEUR-WS.org for online publication.

We kindly ask you to:

  • Edit your manuscript taking into account the reviewers' comments and the feedback you received at the workshop.
    • The minimum length of a CEUR paper should be 5 pages, with approximately 2500 characters (= 380-400 words) per page.
  • Prepare the final version in LaTeX or MS Word using the CEURART 1-column template:
    • LaTeX users can find the template here and on Overleaf: https://www.overleaf.com/read/qbzwymmhjgfm
      • This template is read-only. To create your own editable copy, click on the menu icon (top left corner) and select “copy project”.
        Alternatively, you can select “download source”.
    • MS Word users can find the template here
  • Fill in and sign the author agreement to publish as open-access on CEUR-WS.org here. Please sign the form by hand on paper and send us a scanned copy.
    • If you included copyrighted third party material, please let us know and we will provide you with the appropriate author agreement.
    • If you are unable to print and scan the agreement form, you can fill it in on your computer and place a hand-signed statement below the form and then take a photo of it, as illustrated in this example: http://ceur-ws.org/agreement-paper1.jpg.
  • Send us the camera-ready version of your paper in PDF format and the signed agreement form by sending an email to
    DA DETERMINARE
    by May 23, 2022 (HARD DEADLINE).

    • Please notice that:
    • Papers will only be included in the proceedings if we receive their final versions and the corresponding agreement by the above-mentioned deadline.
    • CEUR-WS.org requires all the submissions to fulfill a few formal rules, e.g., regarding the minimum volume size. Should we fail to meet such requirements, we reserve the opportunity to consider alternative courses of action.
    If you have any questions, please feel free to contact us.

    The workshop organizers
